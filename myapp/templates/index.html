The Internet

We live in a world where the internet and the world wide web have, in the matter of only three decades, shifted from being at the forefront of a new frontier of communication technology to being for most people an incredibly unremarkable part of our culture and daily life. Many once held an optimism that the internet would, for example, revolutionise work and office life, and create active, engaged citizens instead of the passive subjects of the broadcast media age (Poster, 1995), or lead to the creation of alternative communities, worlds and even identities free from the prejudices of offline society (Rheingold, 1993)
